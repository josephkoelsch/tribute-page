Maryam Mirzakhani

The first woman to win the Field's Medal

Biographical Information

  • 1977 -Born in Tehran, Iran.
  • As a child she participated in the National Organization for Development of Exceptional Talents in Iran.
  • In her Junior and Senior years she won the Gold Medal for mathematics in the Iranian National Olympiad.
  • In 1994 she won th gold medal at the International Mathematics Olympiad in Hong Kong--the first Iranian woman to do so.
  • In 1998 she was in a scary bus accident at a conference in which seven other Iranian mathematicians perished.
  • In 1999 she earned her Degree from Sharif University of Technology.
  • In 2004 she earned her PhD from Harvard University.
  • In 2008 she married another mathematician named Jan Vondracht. They would have a daughter.
  • In 2009 she became a professor of mathematics at Stanford University.
  • In 2014 she was awarded the Field's Medal.
  • In 2017 she died of cancer.

Mathematical Contributions

Most of Mirzakhani's work was focused on the abstract mathematics of moduli spaces and Reimann Surfaces. She studies shapes like donuts and double-donuts to create models to predict the path of an object as it traveled through this space.
